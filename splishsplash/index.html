Splish Splash


A good shower can be exhilarating, keeping you vibrant throughout the day. On the contrary, a bad shower experience can ruin your day. In this discussion, one of the existing models of shower/bath interface and design is reviewed and possible improvements are proposed for improved user experience and satisfaction.

Overall layout:




Closeups:

Shower faucet or showerhead:




- Can be rotated to control the type of spray (spray selections like wide or narrow).
- Can be tilted to change the direction of spray.


Shower trim:




- Can be rotated to change the water temperature. Extreme left and right – hot and cold respectively; intermittent positions correspond to intermittent temperatures.
- Can change the speed and amount of water. Pull out the trim to increase the water flow; push the trim all the way in to cut off the water supply.
- The knob has a removable cap. This cap can be used as a cover to block the drain hole (for bath).


Tub faucet (tap) with diverter:




- Simple tap with diverter on top. The diverter can be pulled up to use the shower and pushed down to use the tap.


Indicators of System State


- The position of the diverter indicates which water outlet is being used.
- The positions of the shower trim (clockwise position as well as its projection from the wall) indicate the water temperature and amount of water flow.
- The tilt of the showerhead specifies the direction of water spray.


Sequence of Actions

For Comfortable Shower

  • Pull the shower trim all the way out.
  • Set the water to the desired temperature by feeling the water coming out of the tap.
  • Pull the diverter up to use the shower.
  • Tilt the showerhead to the desired direction.
  • Rotate the showerhead to switch through various spray types and select the desired one.

For Comfortable Bath

  • Pull the shower trim all the way out.
  • Set the water to the desired temperature by feeling the water coming out of the tap.
  • Pull the removable cap from the shower trim and use it to block the drain hole.
  • Wait till the water is up to the desired level and push the shower trim all the way in to cut off the water.

Uses of Shower/Bath


The shower/bath is usually used to clean up, giving bath to kids or in some cases to cool down and relax. Rare uses include cleaning boots or washing the dog.


Usability


Usability corresponds to the factors that refer to promptness with which users learn to use something, the efficiency they attain while making use of it, how easy it is for them to remember how to use it, how error-prone it is and the level of satisfaction that they attain from using it.


New Users


Pros:


The shower design makes it easier for new users to figure out how to use the showerhead to change the spray direction and the markers on the showerhead assist the user in changing the type of spray. The user interface of the shower trim makes it easier for the users to adjust the water temperature – the initials H and C are straightforward and can be related to Hot and Cold respectively.


Cons:


The main flaw in the interface comes up when the user tries to start the water flow. The fact that the shower trim can be pulled out to start the water is not apparent to the new user – the knob gives the sense that it can be rotated to change the water temperature but the fact that it can also be used to change the amount of water by pull-and-push motion is not obvious.
The other significant problem with the interface is the mapping of the diverter. As the diverter is placed on top of the tap, it gives the false sense that it is somehow related to controlling the tap instead of its intended use which is to divert the water flow from tap to shower or vice versa.
Lastly, the fact that the cap on the shower trim can be used to block the drain hole for bath is not evident to a new user – the design is not easily discernable.


Regular Users


Pros:


The user is required to rotate the shower trim as opposed to using a sliding control or a switch to change the water temperature. Rotating the shower trim requires conscious effort on the part of the user as he/she needs to apply torque on the knob – this ensures that there is no change in the temperature due to accidental movements of the user. Also, the change in the water temperature is gradual, this makes certain that the user is relatively safe from sudden outbursts of extremely hot or cold water.

  1. The water flows out of shower (upper faucet) and tap (lower faucet) when the diverter is pulled up and pushed down respectively.
  2. The water comes out when the shower trim is pulled out and stops when it is pushed in.

These two mappings of cause-and-effect movements align with our sensorimotor perception of the world.

The positioning of the controls on the showerhead and shower trim makes sense – the mapping of these controls correspond to the physical aspects they control. For example, the shower trim is placed between the showerhead and tap – this arrangement makes sense as it controls both.


Cons:


In case the user slips, there are no accommodations for support such as handles to regain the balance. Also, the surface of the bathtub is smooth, which results in increased cases of slip-and-fall.
The cap on the shower trim cannot be removed with ease; the design can be improved to make it easily removable.


Accessibility


Accessibility corresponds to the factors which enable all types of users viz., users with disabilities, kids or people from other parts of the world to perceive, understand and use something effectively and efficiently. It is a subset of usability.


Pros:


Almost all controls except the ones that correspond to the showerhead are designed to be within the reach of kids and shorter people.


Cons:


The design doesn’t include any kind of support for the elderly such as handholds or handles.
The shower/bath design doesn’t include accommodations for the disabled or users from other parts of the world (language support).


Improvements


All the improvements are proposed while taking sensorimotor perception of things into consideration. They are listed as follows:





General Features


The shower trim is redesigned so that the controls for the temperature and amount of water flow are differentiated. This facilitates ease of perception. The knobs for controlling the temperature and the rate of water flow are modified to incorporate handles to enhance the ease of use.
The diverter is placed between the showerhead and tap so that its position makes sense.
The type of spray is cannot be perceived by the user unless the shower is on, it can be fixed by using a marker or indicator of some kind that shows the type of spray.
A separate switch is added for the function of draining out or blocking the water in the tub.
The floor of the bathtub is intentionally designed to be rough so that it prevents accidents like slip-and-fall.


Accessibility Features


Different types of users are taken into account while making design improvements for accessibility. They are listed as follows:
Supports for the elderly like handholds and handles are added.
Braille and symbols are also used in addition to English to overcome the barriers of language.
A seat can be incorporated into the design to accommodate the physically challenged.
